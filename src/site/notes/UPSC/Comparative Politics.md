# Introduction 
- Comparative Politics has a messy centre.
- Focuses on comparision and the 'comparative method' as the method of political inquiry 
- It is relatively a new field dating from the post secon world war period. It is a field that is difficul to define. 
- It has undergone many changes and reached a plateau by the 1980s beyond which it could not move. 

# What is comparative politics and its evolution 
- The goal of comparative politics is to encompas the major political similarities and differences between countries. 
- The task is to develop some perspective on the mixture of constants and variabillity which charactrices the world's governments and the context in which they operate. 
- comparative government term is quite old - it is referred as traditional approach
- The term comparative politics as mentioned above is relatively new. Add this is referred as worded approach
- 1955 R.C. Macridisc differentiated the two when he pointed out that the traditional approach was **non comparative**, **descriptive**, **parochial**, **static** and **monographic**
